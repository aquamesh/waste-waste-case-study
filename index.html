The Plant

Every gauge is reading. Nobody owns the decision.

A mid-sized activated-sludge plant treats 5 to 15 million gallons a day, running blowers around the clock to keep dissolved oxygen in range while chasing ammonia, nitrate, and solids through the same treatment train. Aeration alone can consume 40 to 70 percent of a plant's total electricity, and energy overall makes up 25 to 40 percent of the operating budget — yet blower speed is still often set by schedule, not by real, continuously measured oxygen demand.

The plant already owns all the usual equipment below. That sounds complete. It is not. Every tool shows data, but nothing turns that data into the right decision.

Example Operating Event

A storm event hits the collection system

Without AquaMesh

Overcorrect to stay safe.
A rain event drives a surge of flow and ammonia into the plant. To stay safe, the operator ramps blowers to maximum. It's the common move, but blowers are routinely over-run during these swings, and more air doesn't fix an ammonia slug. The plant stays compliant, but wastes money: more energy, more chemical, more strain on the clarifier.

With AquaMesh

AquaMesh catches the odd fingerprint early, recognizes a storm-driven flow and ammonia surge, and predicts how it will hit the clarifier and disinfection stage. It recommends:

  1. Ramp blower speed to the load the water actually needs
  2. Hold and equalize the incoming surge ahead of the aeration basin
  3. Watch clarifier output to confirm solids capture held
  4. Escalate only if effluent risk continues
